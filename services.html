Aggie Competitive Programming Club

About ACPC

Aggie Competitive Programming Club (ACPC) fosters a community for Texas A&M students interested in learning and teaching competitive programming techniques and algorithmic problem-solving skills. Through weekly meetings and semesterly programming contests, ACPC shares knowledge and creates a collaborative learning environment for A&M students.

Role and Involvement

As a Problemsetting Officer at ACPC, I propose and review coding problems for our weekly club meetings and the TAMU Programming Contests. My responsibilities as a Problem Setter include designing the Competitive Programming curriculum and creating practice problems to challenge our members during weekly sessions. Additionally, I contribute to organizing the TAMU programming contests, providing problem solutions to enhance the educational experience for all participants.

Experience

Last semester, ACPC successfully organized the TAMU Fall 2023 programming contest. As part of the preparation team, I helped create challenging problems for the contest. We hosted the event on the Codeforces platform, with onsite participation from 101 students representing Texas A&M University and the University of Texas at Austin.

The Big Event 2023

About Big Event

The Big Event at Texas A&M University is a student-led community service project known as the largest one-day, student-run service initiative in the nation. Each spring, Aggie students dedicate their time and effort to this event as a gesture of appreciation, undertaking service projects to give back to the community. The Big Event maintains a consistent message and mission: to express their heartfelt gratitude to the residents of Bryan and College Station.

Involvement

During my participation in The Big Event, I was part of a team of volunteers assigned to assist an elderly homeowner in College Station. Our day began at 9 AM, armed with tools and a shared determination to create a positive impact. We engaged in a range of activities, from clearing debris to decorating the garden space. Our team worked cohesively, ensuring every detail was attended to with care and respect for the homeowner's space and preferences. By 3 PM, our collective effort had resulted in a revitalized garden, bringing a renewed sense of joy to the homeowner.

Experience

This experience was more than just a service project; it was about meeting new friends and spending time contributing to the community. I am struck by the profound sense of connection and community spirit that the experience fostered. The Big Event allowed me to demonstrate the core values of service and gratitude, contributing to an initiative that stands as a hallmark of Texas A&M's commitment to the community.
